# Nibi Walks — Proposed Navigation Scheme

**Based on:** Discovery Steps 1–2, 4–8
**Prepared for:** Nibi Walks / IPTF website redesign

---

## Guiding Principles Behind This Structure

The research is clear: this site is not a public event listing — it is a prayer. Every navigation decision below flows from that. The structure is designed to:

- Honor Indigenous leadership and make it visible and specific (Sharon Day, IPTF, named people)
- Create clear pathways for each distinct audience without mixing their needs
- Elevate the most-visited and most-important content (upcoming walks, protocol, donation)
- Retire or restructure what no longer reflects who Nibi Walks is today (the "Every Day" tab, outdated media buried in About, opaque blog)
- Never pursue size of audience for its own sake — invite meaningful participation

## Detailed Structure

### 1. The Walk
*What Nibi Walks are, who leads them, and why they exist.*

This section answers the first and most important user task: "Learn what Nibi Walks are." It grounds every visitor — whether a curious newcomer, a journalist, or a returning supporter — in the spiritual and cultural authority behind the work before they act on anything else.

- **About Nibi Walks** — Purpose, origins, the prayer, the eighth fire prophecy
- **Indigenous Leadership** — Named profiles of Sharon Day, IPTF, and key community leaders (not "a group of Indigenous women")
- **The Waters** — Structured entries for each body of water walked: Indigenous name, location, significance, related walks
- **Walk History & Archive** — Past walk records: where, when, key moments, media, reflections

> *Note: The About page was flagged as not reflecting who Nibi Walks is today. This restructured section should be rewritten from scratch with current leadership input.*

---

### 2. Join a Walk
*Practical and cultural preparation for people who want to walk.*

Serves both **current walkers** (logistics, updates) and **potential walkers** (is this for me? how do I prepare?). Each upcoming walk gets its own dedicated page rather than being stacked into a single list.

- **Upcoming Walks** — Index of upcoming walks; each walk has its own page (dates, route, starting points, organizers, map, support needs, related updates)
- **Follow an Active Walk** — Real-time or near-real-time walk updates, GPS tracking map
- **Protocol & Guidelines** — How to show up respectfully; broken into specific, repeatable protocol guidance items (not one overwhelming page)
- **How to Prepare** — What to bring, how to show up self-sufficient, what to expect
- **Music & Language** — Language references, songs connected to the walks

> *Note: The current "Upcoming" page was flagged for a disproportionately large photo and lack of findability. Individual walk pages resolve this. Protocol page was flagged as needing significant work.*

---

### 3. Walk in Spirit
*For people who cannot walk physically but want to participate meaningfully from afar.*

This is an entirely new primary section — currently there is no clear home for spiritual walkers on the site, despite this being one of the largest and most motivated audiences. The guiding principle that "site visitors are able to participate in the prayer with the walkers from afar" makes this a top-level destination, not a footnote.

- **Pray With Us** — How to participate as a spiritual walker; what this means, what is welcome
- **Prayer Prompts** — Recurring items tied to active walks, seasons, or specific bodies of water
- **Share the Walk** — How to share the message appropriately; approved language, what not to photograph or post
- **Off-Season Practices** — Seasonal resources to stay connected to water care between walks

---

### 4. Stories & Teachings
*Reflections, spiritual teachings, media, and the deeper meaning of the work.*

This consolidates what is currently scattered across Blog, Every Day, and Media (buried in About). It is organized by content type, not by date, so the most meaningful pieces surface regardless of when they were published.

- **Reflections** — From walkers, Sharon, approved community voices; short and long-form
- **Teachings** — Repeatable spiritual and cultural framing; values, the eight fire prophecy, water relationship
- **Walker Stories** — First-person accounts of what a walk meant; more personal than a general reflection
- **Media** — Podcasts, videos, press features, interviews — curated and tagged by topic and recency
- **Nibi Songs** — Information about the music, how to listen, and how to purchase

> *Note: Videos and podcasts were flagged as outdated. This section should be freshened with recent media as a priority. The current "Every Day" tab content was flagged as feeling old — this replaces it with a structure that can stay current.*

---

### 5. Support
*Clear, specific pathways to give financially or materially.*

Donors were flagged as needing: a clear giving path, transparency about how donations are used, context on IPTF's role, and an understanding that walkers are unpaid and taking leave from jobs. Material support providers need to know exactly who to contact and what is useful. This section serves both.

- **Donate** — Active donation campaigns and fund needs (tied to specific walks or goals); giving tools; relationship to IPTF explained
- **Material Support** — Housing, food, transportation, supplies; who to contact; how the flexibility of Nibi Walks works
- **Shop** — Products, CDs, educational materials, merch
- **Events** — Exhibits, fundraisers, community gatherings, teaching sessions

> *Note: Nibi Songs currently lives as a standalone top-level nav item. Moving it here — with its own rich page under Stories & Teachings as well — makes it more findable without making it feel siloed.*

---

### 6. Connect
*Stay in relationship with Nibi Walks beyond any single walk.*

Serves off-season walkers, journalists, and anyone who wants to stay plugged in. Also the right home for press resources, which currently have no clear location.

- **Newsletter** — Sign up; what to expect
- **News & Updates** — Organizational announcements, milestones, new partnerships, schedule releases (not tied to a specific walk)
- **For Media & Journalists** — Approved language, bios, media assets, press releases, journalist resources; how to represent the walks accurately
- **Exhibit Info** — Information about exhibits and installations connected to the work

## A Note on Naming

Several labels in this proposal use plain, direct language rather than evocative language (e.g., "Join a Walk" rather than "Walk With Water"). This is intentional for the navigation level — clarity reduces friction for first-time visitors. The spiritual framing and voice should live in the page content, headings, and imagery rather than in the nav labels, where ambiguity creates confusion (see: "Every Day").

The exception is **"Walk in Spirit"** — this label is intentionally evocative because it names something that has no plain-language equivalent and because it signals to the right audience immediately.
