# Nibi Walks — Navigation Rationale

The proposed navigation is built around a single insight from the discovery process: this site is a prayer, not a public event listing. Every structural decision follows from that.

---

**The Walk** (formerly About) is reframed to lead with purpose and people — the spiritual meaning of the walks, the named Indigenous leaders behind the work, the waters themselves, and the historical record. The current About page was flagged as not reflecting who Nibi Walks is today. This section should be rebuilt from that foundation.

**Join a Walk** elevates preparation and protocol to the same level as logistics. Potential walkers need to know what is expected of them culturally before they show up — not find that buried in a submenu. Each upcoming walk also gets its own dedicated page, resolving the current problem of too many walks stacked in one place with no room for detail.

**Walk in Spirit** is new and fills a real gap. Spiritual walkers — people who cannot attend physically but want to participate meaningfully in the prayer from afar — are one of the site's largest and most motivated audiences. They currently have no clear home. This section gives them one.

**Stories & Teachings** consolidates what is now scattered across Blog, Every Day, and Media into a single section organized by content type rather than date. This means the most meaningful reflections, teachings, and media surface based on what they are — not when they were posted. It also creates a path to retire the "Every Day" section, which was flagged as outdated and unclear.

**Support** brings together donation, material support, and the shop under one roof. Donors told us they need more than a button — they need to understand IPTF's role, that walkers take unpaid leave, and how their contribution actually helps. This section is designed to give them that context alongside a clear path to give.

**Connect** creates a proper home for press and media resources (currently nonexistent), organizational news, exhibit information, and the newsletter — things that don't fit neatly elsewhere but matter to key audiences including journalists, off-season walkers, and community partners.

---

*This structure should be reviewed with Sharon Day and IPTF leadership before any implementation decisions are made.*
